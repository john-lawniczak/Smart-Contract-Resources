## Market Manipulation Techniques (Traditional & Crypto)

Understanding these traditional finance manipulation tactics helps identify similar patterns in DeFi/MEV:

### Order Book Manipulation

- **[Quote stuffing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quote_stuffing)** - flooding order books with fake orders
  - **Crypto equivalent**: Gas price manipulation, mempool flooding

- **[Spoofing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spoofing_(finance))** - placing fake orders to move prices
  - **Crypto equivalent**: Wash trading, fake liquidity in AMMs

### Information Asymmetry

- **[Front-running](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/f/frontrunning.asp)** - TradFi version of MEV sandwich attacks
  - **Direct parallel**: MEV searchers front-running user transactions

- **[Payment for Order Flow (PFOF)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Payment_for_order_flow)** - selling trade information
  - **Crypto equivalent**: Private mempools, MEV-Boost relayers

## Financial Regulation & Systemic Risk

### Regulatory Frameworks

- **[Glass-Steagall Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glass%E2%80%93Steagall_legislation)** - separation of investment/commercial banking
  - **Lessons for DeFi**: Protocol separation and risk isolation

- **[Regulation SHO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naked_short_selling#Regulation_SHO)** - naked short selling rules
  - **Relevant to**: Synthetic assets, flash loans, and over-leveraged positions

- **[Regulatory capture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulatory_capture)** - how industries influence their regulators
  - **Important for**: DAO governance and protocol governance design
